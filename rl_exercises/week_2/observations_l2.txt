In Level 1, we had access to the full MDP model, meaning we knew the transition probabilities T(s,a,s') 
and the reward function R(s,a). This allowed us to compute expectations over all possible next states.

For example, in Policy Evaluation (used in Policy Iteration), we compute:

    Vπ(s) = R(s, π(s)) + γ * Σ_{s'} T(s, π(s), s') * Vπ(s')

Similarly, in Value Iteration, we compute:

    V*(s) = max_a [ R(s,a) + γ * Σ_{s'} T(s,a,s') * V*(s') ]

Both equations require summing over all possible next states s' weighted by the transition probabilities T(s,a,s'). 
This means we must know the full transition model T and the reward function R in order to compute these expectations.

Because of this, we cannot compute the expectation term Σ T(s,a,s') * V(s'), which is essential in both Policy Iteration 
and Value Iteration. Therefore, these algorithms can no longer be applied directly.
